# ADA Pool Compliance Guide for Service Technicians

## Overview
The Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) requires public pools and spas to be accessible. Understanding these requirements helps technicians identify compliance issues and advise commercial clients.

---

## 1. ADA Applicability

### Who Must Comply

**Must Comply (Title III):**
- Hotels and motels
- Apartment complexes (common area pools)
- Homeowners associations (community pools)
- Health clubs and gyms
- Country clubs
- Recreation centers
- Schools and universities
- Public parks
- Resorts

**Exempt:**
- Private residential pools
- Strictly private clubs (limited membership)

### Compliance Deadlines

- New construction: Must comply from inception
- Existing facilities: Required to be accessible
- "Readily achievable" standard applies to existing facilities

---

## 2. Means of Entry Requirements

### Large Pools (300+ linear feet of pool wall)

**Required:** TWO accessible means of entry
- At least ONE must be a pool lift OR sloped entry
- Second can be: pool lift, sloped entry, transfer wall, transfer platform, or pool stairs

### Small Pools (Under 300 linear feet)

**Required:** ONE accessible means of entry
- Must be a pool lift OR sloped entry

### Spas

**Required:** ONE accessible means of entry
- Pool lift, transfer wall, or transfer platform
- If spa is attached to pool with accessible entry, additional entry may not be required

---

## 3. Pool Lift Requirements

### Technical Specifications

**Seat:**
- 16 inches minimum width
- Located over pool edge, not on deck
- Must submerge to 18 inches below water surface

**Capacity:**
- Minimum 300 pound capacity

**Controls:**
- Operable with one hand
- No tight grasping, pinching, or twisting required
- Unattended operation capable

**Armrests:**
- Required on both sides
- Removable or folding to allow transfer

**Footrests:**
- Required
- Must move with seat

### Operational Requirements

**Lift must be:**
- Operational during pool hours
- Available without staff assistance
- Maintained in working condition

**Location:**
- Clear deck space required (36" × 48" minimum)
- Accessible path to lift

### Common Compliance Issues

| Issue | Problem | Solution |
|-------|---------|----------|
| Lift not working | ADA violation | Immediate repair |
| Lift stored away | Must be pool-side | Leave in position |
| Requires staff to operate | Must be user-operable | Check controls |
| Battery dead | Not accessible | Charge/replace |
| No clear deck space | Access blocked | Clear area |

---

## 4. Sloped Entry (Zero-Depth Entry)

### Requirements

**Slope:**
- Maximum 1:12 (8.33%)
- Level landing at top and bottom

**Width:**
- Minimum 36 inches wide
- Full depth width (not converging)

**Handrails:**
- Required on both sides
- Must extend entire length
- 34-38 inches above surface

**Surface:**
- Slip-resistant
- Color-contrasted edge markings

---

## 5. Transfer Walls, Platforms & Stairs

### Transfer Wall
- Height: 16-19 inches above deck
- Depth: 12-16 inches
- Length: 60 inches minimum
- Clear deck space required
- Grab bars required

### Transfer Platform
- Height: 16-19 inches above deck
- Size: 19" deep × 24" wide minimum
- Clear deck space required
- Grab bars may be required

### Pool Stairs
- Must meet accessible stair standards
- Handrails on both sides
- Slip-resistant treads
- Can serve as secondary means only

---

## 6. Inspection Checklist

### Pool Lift Inspection

**Physical Condition:**
- [ ] Lift present and positioned at pool
- [ ] Seat in good condition
- [ ] Armrests functioning (removable/folding)
- [ ] Footrest attached and operational
- [ ] No visible damage or rust

**Operational Check:**
- [ ] Battery charged
- [ ] Controls functional
- [ ] Lift raises and lowers smoothly
- [ ] Seat submerges to 18" depth
- [ ] Emergency lowering works

**Surrounding Area:**
- [ ] Clear deck space (36" × 48")
- [ ] Accessible route to lift
- [ ] No obstructions

### Document Findings
- Note any issues
- Report to facility manager
- Recommend corrective action
- Follow up on unresolved items

---

## 7. Communicating with Commercial Clients

### When You Notice Compliance Issues

**Do:**
- Document the issue
- Inform the responsible party
- Explain the requirement simply
- Recommend they consult ADA specialist

**Don't:**
- Ignore the issue
- Claim expertise you don't have
- Make legal determinations
- Attempt to fix ADA equipment

### Sample Communication

"I noticed the pool lift [specific issue]. ADA requires pool lifts to be operational during pool hours. I recommend having this looked at promptly. Would you like me to note this in my service report?"

### When to Refer

Refer to ADA specialist or pool contractor for:
- Installation of new accessible features
- Major lift repairs
- Compliance assessments
- Legal questions

---

## 8. Maintenance Considerations

### Pool Lift Maintenance (General)

**Daily (Facility Responsibility):**
- Verify lift is operational
- Check battery indicator
- Ensure positioned at pool

**Weekly:**
- Operational test
- Visual inspection for damage
- Clean and lubricate per manufacturer

**Monthly:**
- Check all bolts and connections
- Battery maintenance
- Detailed operational check

**Annually:**
- Professional inspection
- Replace worn components
- Verify compliance

### Your Role as Service Technician

**Include in Service:**
- Visual check of lift condition
- Note if lift is missing or stored away
- Report non-operational equipment
- Include in service documentation

**Do Not:**
- Repair or modify ADA equipment
- Move lifts without authorization
- Certify ADA compliance

## 9. Quick Reference

### Entry Requirements Summary

| Pool Type | Size | Required Entry |
|-----------|------|----------------|
| Large pool | 300+ ft wall | 2 entries (1 must be lift or sloped) |
| Small pool | Under 300 ft | 1 entry (lift or sloped) |
| Spa | Any | 1 entry (lift, transfer wall, or platform) |

### Pool Lift Quick Check
1. Is lift present at pool? ✓/✗
2. Is lift operational? ✓/✗
3. Does it submerge to 18"? ✓/✗
4. Are controls accessible? ✓/✗
5. Is deck space clear? ✓/✗

### Red Flags to Report
- Lift removed from pool area
- "Out of Order" sign for extended period
- Lift requiring staff assistance only
- Physical damage to lift
- Missing components
